## Detailed Summary of Jagdev Ram Uraon

Jagdev Ram Uraon was a renowned tribal social worker, educationist, and national-level organizer who dedicated his entire life to the upliftment of tribal communities in India. He was born on 9 October 1949 in Komdo village near Jashpur, in the region that is now part of Chhattisgarh. His father’s name was Aghanu Ram.

From his early years, Jagdev Ram Ji showed qualities of discipline, simplicity, and leadership. During his high school education in Jashpur, he came into contact with the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS). He traveled from village to village on bicycles with local volunteers, which helped him understand the social and educational problems faced by tribal communities.

In 1968, he joined the Vanvasi Kalyan Ashram, an organization working for the welfare and development of tribal society. He received formal Physical Education training at Shivpuri and later became a physical education teacher in the organization’s school. Along with sports and discipline, he also taught Sanskrit and inspired students through moral values and cultural education. While working as a teacher, he completed his Master’s degree in History.

During the Emergency imposed in India in 1975, Jagdev Ram Ji was imprisoned in Raigarh Jail because of his ideological and organizational activities. Even imprisonment could not weaken his dedication. After his release, he immediately resumed social work with even greater determination.

One of the most important phases of his life began when Bala Saheb Deshpande started traveling across India with him after the organization expanded nationally in 1978. Jagdev Ram Ji visited remote villages, forests, hills, and tribal settlements throughout the country. Despite holding high responsibilities, he lived with extraordinary simplicity. He usually traveled carrying only two pairs of clothes and an RSS uniform.

His leadership abilities, humility, and deep understanding of tribal society impressed Bala Saheb Deshpande greatly. In 1993, during the All India Workers’ Conference in Cuttack, Odisha, Bala Saheb publicly declared Jagdev Ram Ji as his successor. In 1995, after Bala Saheb’s death, Jagdev Ram Ji became the National President of Vanvasi Kalyan Ashram.

As president, he expanded the organization’s activities across India. Though he himself belonged to the Uraon tribe, he always worked for the welfare of all tribal communities without discrimination. He believed in unity, cultural pride, self-reliance, and national integration. He encouraged tribal communities to preserve their traditions while also progressing through education and social awareness.

Jagdev Ram Ji possessed immense knowledge of tribal culture, Indian traditions, history, and scriptures. He regularly delivered inspiring speeches filled with historical references, cultural understanding, and practical wisdom. His strong memory and deep study of books made him a respected intellectual among social workers and scholars.

In 2012, he was specially invited to the International Conference of the Elders of Ancient Traditions organized by the International Centre for Cultural Studies at Dev Sanskriti University. During this event, the University of World Ancient Traditions & Cultural Heritage, USA honored him with an honorary doctorate for his contribution to tribal welfare and cultural preservation.

He received many honors, including:

* Tribal Service Award by the Government of Chhattisgarh (2011)
* Late Bhaurao Deoras Service Award
* Appreciation from various social and cultural institutions

Despite national recognition, Jagdev Ram Ji remained extremely humble and detached from material comforts. He spent most of his personal resources on:

* Books and educational activities
* Temple development
* Support for priests and social workers
* Religious and cultural programs

He often traveled in ordinary sleeper-class train compartments and avoided luxury. He remained unmarried throughout his life and fully devoted himself to social service.

People who met him often described him as calm, disciplined, affectionate, and spiritually elevated through conduct rather than rituals. Former Jashpur ruler Ranvijay Singh Judev recalled his humility and deep respect toward everyone, regardless of status.

Jagdev Ram Uraon Ji passed away on 15 July 2020. His life continues to inspire thousands of social workers, volunteers, students, and tribal communities across India. He is remembered as a symbol of selfless service, simplicity, cultural pride, and dedication to society.
